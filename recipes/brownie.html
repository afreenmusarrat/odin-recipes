Chocolate Brownie

A chocolate brownie, or simply a brownie, is a chocolate baked dessert bar. Brownies come in a variety of forms and may be either fudgy or cakey, depending on their density. Brownies often have a glossy "skin" on their upper crust. They may also include nuts, frosting, chocolate chips, or other ingredients. A variation can be made with brown sugar and vanilla rather than chocolate in the batter and is called a blond brownie or blondie.

Brownies may be accompanied by a glass of milk, served warm with ice cream, topped with whipped cream, or sprinkled with powdered sugar. In most homes, they are common homemade treats and are also popular in restaurants, ice cream parlors, and coffeehouses. This recipe makes the most fudgy chocolate brownies with a perfect glossy upper crust. They are goeey on the inside, crisp on the edges and will melt in your mouth as you eat.

You'll find a detailed ingredient list and step-by-step instructions in the recipe card below.

Recipe Card

Ingredients:

  • 50g dark chocolate, melted
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup + 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 50g chocolate chunks

Steps:

  1. Whisk the egg, sugar, and vanilla together till the sugar dissolves.
  2. In a pan melt the chocolate and butter together on low flame. Let it cool down.
  3. Then pour the chocolate mixture into the whisked egg batter.
  4. Sift the flour, cocoa powder and salt into the batter.
  5. Fold in the chocolate chunks.
  6. Pour the batter into a parchment lined mould.
  7. Bake in the oven at 170°C for 25-30 minutes.
  8. Let the brownie cool down for 30 minutes before slicing.
